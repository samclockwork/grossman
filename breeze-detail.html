A camera installed atop a Minneapolis office building was programmed to take 1,000 evenly spaced exposures of the sky each day for a year. In this natural narrative of 365,000 photos, as in Breeze Avenue itself, nothing is captured except for what the wind occupies or what occupies the wind.

Pop-Up Torah

Conceived as the longest lasting artwork in history, this Poetic Object began with the burial of a 5-inchcorundum ball, engraved with the Ten Commandments, close to the summit of Mount Princeton, a 14,000 foot-high peak in southern Colorado. After approximately 20 million years, as a result of natural forces, the Torah Ballwill emerge undamaged from the eroded peak and bear the Ten Commandments down the mountain.

Nearly fifty years in the making, Richard Grossman’s Breeze Avenue, invites readers to embark on a mind-bending journey into a vast alternate universe, an ephemeral, heavenly realm that operates according to its own natural laws and principles. Comprising 44 trillion bytes of information, Breeze Avenue’s components are housed online in the plenum, a constantly shifting channel of windborne voices, words and images that form up into 50 discrete elements and their offshoots.

Breeze Avenue’s elements draw on such wildly disparate disciplines as poetry, geology, film, philosophy, finance, theater, cosmology, music, archaeology, literary theory, Deaf culture, architecture, zoology, lexicography, politics, art history, engineering, medicine, photography, and religion, among others. In addition to English, works are produced in Latin, Yiddish, Orhkon, Hebrew, Fraser, Sanskrit, Japanese, Chinese, hieroglyphs, American Sign Language, the International Phonetic Alphabet and various original forms of symbolic notation.

The plenum is in a constant process of decay and regeneration that occurs in 36.5-day cycles, ten times a year, over a period of five centuries. At the beginning of each cycle, the plenum presents a perfectly ordered world that immediately starts to devolve. At the midpoint of the cycle, it reaches a state of utter chaos with the sequence of 3,000,000 pages completely disordered, before the pattern reverses and the plenum coalesces into a unique, perfectly ordered universe.

At that moment, the plenum emanates two works available to be viewed online for free: a 3,000,000-page Eternal Volume and a 5,000-page Evanescent Novel.

Eternal Volumes remain permanently available online as they become installed, one per cycle, in the site’s Eternal Reading Room. Each is also available to be acquired as an NFT.

An Evanescent Novel, by contrast, has a relatively short lifespan. With every new plenary emanation, the preceding novel vanishes and is memorialized only if during its 36.5-day online existence a visitor to the site opts to acquire it as an NFT. (The sole exception is the first emanation—Breeze Avenue: The Initial State—which is being offered as a common reference document in unchanging print and eBook editions.) Within each Evanescent Novel 30 portals offer the opportunity to access the plenum and watch its components flow by.

Breeze Avenue also includes 32 discrete books, which are being published over time in print and eBook editions, as well as a group of Poetic Objects, artworks in a wide range of media that move the project beyond the page and the screen into the real world.
